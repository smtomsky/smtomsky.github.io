Images

These images are of the lensed bulges of Active Galactic Nuclei generated by code written by myself and my professor, Matt Wiesner at Benedictine University as part of research with the Legacy Survey of Space and Time Dark Energy Science Collaboration. They depict simulations of Gravitationally Lensed Galaxies. A gravitational lens creates a distorted image of a more distant object as light is bent around a nearer massive body. This distortion appears as a ring, arc or multiple images around the image of the lensing body.

The primary use of such observations lies in the gravitational lensing time delays that occur when an object of variable brightness (like an AGN or supernova) is lensed. The multiple images of the object will change brightness at different times. The delay in brightness change depends on Hubble Parameter, H and thus time delays can be used to measure H, the expansion rate of the universe.
